## 4. Keyword Intent Analysis

### `corporate training prices`
**Intent:** Informational + Commercial Investigation
Buyer is in **early consideration** — researching what training costs before deciding on a vendor. They want:
- Industry benchmarks ("how much does corporate training cost?")
- Factors that affect pricing
- Comparison of pricing models
- Transparent numbers or at least ranges

**Current gap:** Page jumps straight to Edstellar's packages without addressing the buyer's "how much does this cost?" question in an educational way.

### `corporate training packages`
**Intent:** Commercial Investigation
Buyer is in **mid-funnel consideration** — actively evaluating package structures. They want:
- What's included in a typical package
- How packages are structured (hours, learners, duration)
- Comparison of tiers
- What makes one package better than another
- ROI / value justification

**Current gap:** The packages exist but there's no "how to choose" guide, no ROI framing, no comparison with industry norms.

### Supporting Long-Tail Keywords to Target (add as subheadings)
- "how much does corporate training cost per employee"
- "corporate training cost per employee"
- "instructor-led training prices"
- "corporate training program cost"
- "employee training packages for companies"
- "bulk training packages for organizations"
- "virtual corporate training packages"
- "onsite training pricing"

## 5. New Sections to Add — Prioritized

### SECTION A: Industry Pricing Benchmark Context
**Why:** Targets the informational intent of "corporate training prices." Buyers search for "how much does training cost" before they search for vendors.
**What it contains:**
- Industry average: $774 per learner (ATD 2024 data)
- Cost by company size: Large ($398/employee), Mid ($739), Small ($1,047)
- Training format cost ranges: ILT ($3,000–$7,000/hr), VILT (10–30% less), eLearning ($3,300–$15,800/30 min)
- Edstellar's positioning: "Our packages deliver up to 60% cost savings vs. building an in-house team"
**Design:** Data table or split editorial + stat card layout
**SEO role:** Answers "how much does corporate training cost" directly — pulls informational searchers into the page

---

### SECTION B: Package Comparison Guide ("How to Choose the Right Package")
**Why:** Buyers comparing packages need a decision framework. This targets mid-funnel "corporate training packages" intent.
**What it contains:**
- 3-step guide: Estimate your team size → Calculate training hours needed → Choose your validity window
- Package selector logic (e.g., "< 100 employees → Starter", "100–500 → Growth")
- Common use cases per tier (onboarding, upskilling sprint, annual program)
**Design:** Horizontal step guide or interactive selector
**SEO role:** Ranks for "how to choose corporate training package" long-tail; increases time-on-page

---

### SECTION C: ROI Calculator / Value Framing Section
**Why:** The #1 objection to corporate training pricing is "is it worth it?" Addressing ROI on the pricing page itself removes the objection before it forms.
**What it contains:**
- Stat: "Companies with strong L&D programs are 92% more likely to innovate" (Deloitte)
- Stat: "Employee replacement costs 50–200% of annual salary" — training reduces turnover
- Simple ROI framing: "A 2% improvement in sales conversion from training = $200K extra revenue per $10M team"
- Edstellar packages framed as investment, not cost
**Design:** Navy background section with 3–4 impact stats + one editorial ROI narrative
**SEO role:** Matches "corporate training ROI" and "is corporate training worth the cost" queries; increases page authority signals

---

### SECTION D: What's Included vs. Competitors (Transparent Comparison)
**Why:** Buyers always compare. A comparison table positions Edstellar favorably while keeping the user on-page rather than going to competitor sites.
**What it contains:**
- Table: Edstellar vs. "In-House Training Team" vs. "Ad-Hoc Vendors" vs. "eLearning Platforms"
- Rows: Course breadth, trainer quality, dedicated manager, platform included, pricing flexibility, global delivery
- Edstellar wins on breadth + managed service + flexibility
**Design:** 4-column comparison table with checkmarks (navy) and X marks (red)
**SEO role:** Targets "edstellar vs competitors", "corporate training company comparison" queries; keeps bounced users on page

---

### SECTION E: Delivery Modes & Pricing Explained
**Why:** The current page mentions onsite/virtual in footnotes. A dedicated section targeting "virtual corporate training prices" and "onsite training pricing" captures more keyword variants.
**What it contains:**
- Virtual ILT: What it costs, how it's delivered, best for distributed teams
- Onsite ILT: +30% pricing explained, what's included (travel, accommodation), best for hands-on training
- Blended: Combination options
- Self-paced eLearning: Add-on to packages
**Design:** 3-column card layout or tab section
**SEO role:** Captures "virtual corporate training packages", "onsite training pricing", "blended learning packages" long-tails

---

### SECTION F: Client Outcomes / Mini Case Studies
**Why:** EEAT's "Experience" pillar. Real outcomes from real clients prove the pricing is justified and build trust.
**What it contains:**
- 3 client snapshots: Company name (or anonymized), industry, challenge, package used, outcome achieved
- Stats per story: e.g., "320 employees trained, 94% completion rate, 18% improvement in audit scores"
- Named quote from L&D manager or HR leader
**Design:** Success stories carousel (dark/navy version)
**SEO role:** Adds Experience and Trustworthiness signals; schema-ready for review markup; targets "corporate training results" intent

---

### SECTION G: Trainer Quality Assurance Section
**Why:** A key buyer question is "who delivers the training?" — especially for per-seat priced packages. Trainer quality justifies pricing.
**What it contains:**
- Vetting process: Background check, subject matter test, delivery audit
- Stats: "1,200+ verified trainers", "average 12 years domain experience", "4.8/5 average trainer rating"
- Specialisations: Industry certifications trainers hold (PMP, CISSP, SHRM, etc.)
**Design:** Horizontal 4-stat strip + 3-step vetting process
**SEO role:** Builds Expertise EEAT signal; targets "certified corporate trainers" and "verified training providers" queries

---

### SECTION H: Dedicated Learning Services Manager Explained
**Why:** This is a genuine differentiator that competitors don't offer, but it's buried in the package table. It deserves a standalone section.
**What it contains:**
- Visual showing the LSM's role across the engagement lifecycle (onboarding → scheduling → delivery → retrospective → renewal)
- What the LSM handles: Scheduling, coordination, trainer briefing, feedback collection, reporting
- Quote from an LSM or client about the service
**Design:** Horizontal timeline or 5-step lifecycle strip
**SEO role:** Differentiator content; supports "managed training service" and "dedicated training manager" queries

---

### SECTION I: Industries We Train (Pricing Page Version)
**Why:** Industry-specific training packages are a distinct purchase consideration. Showing coverage by industry increases relevance for industry-qualified buyers.

## 10. Conversion Optimisation Additions

Beyond SEO, the following elements improve conversion rate on the pricing page:

1. **Sticky CTA bar** — "Get a Custom Quote" button that follows the user as they scroll through packages
2. **Package recommendation quiz** — 3-question inline form: team size, training frequency, budget range → recommends a package
3. **Live chat / WhatsApp widget** — visible on pricing page for real-time questions
4. **"Most Popular" badge on Growth package** — already implied, should be more visually prominent
5. **Savings callout on Enterprise** — "Save 20% vs. per-session pricing" in a lime badge
6. **Exit intent popup** — triggers if user starts to leave: "Want us to calculate your package cost? Enter your team size."
7. **Calendly / meeting embed** — "Book a 15-min pricing call" option in the contact section
